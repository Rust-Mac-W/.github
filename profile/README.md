## 📖 Overview

<a href="#rust">Rust</a> is Facepunch Studios' landmark open-world survival multiplayer game — one of the defining titles of the survival genre, placing players on a harsh procedurally generated island with nothing and demanding they survive through resource gathering, crafting, building, and the constant threat from other players and the environment.

The <a href="#rust">survival loop foundation</a> drives all Rust gameplay — gathering raw materials like wood, stone, metal ore, and sulfur from the environment, processing those materials into usable resources, and crafting the items needed to survive and progress. Every player starts with only a rock and a torch, and the path from that starting point to a fortified base with advanced weaponry runs through hours of material gathering, crafting research, and constant risk management. The gap between a freshly spawned player and an established veteran group is intentionally vast, creating the tension and stakes that define the Rust experience. The <a href="#rust">base building system</a> constructs player shelters and fortifications using structural elements like foundations, walls, floors, doors, and frames — building from a small starter shelter to elaborate multi-story compounds with airlock entries, honeycomb structures, tool cupboard placement, and architectural decisions that determine how defensible the base will be against raiding attempts by other players.

The <a href="#rust">raiding and PvP combat</a> is Rust's most distinctive and controversial element — other players may attempt to destroy your base using explosives and enter it to take everything stored inside, while you may do the same to others. This persistent threat from other players creates the intense, high-stakes gameplay environment that makes successful survival in Rust feel genuinely meaningful, and makes loss feel genuinely consequential in a way that games without such stakes cannot produce. The <a href="#rust">crafting tree depth</a> progresses from primitive stone tools through metal and eventually high-quality metal and technical components, with hundreds of craftable items covering weapons, tools, medical items, clothing, base components, and electrical devices.</p>

<a href="#rust">Programmable electronics system</a> allows constructing automated base defenses, lighting systems, sensor-triggered alarms, and complex electronic configurations using in-game circuit components — an optional depth layer that rewards players who invest time in learning the system with sophisticated base automation. <a href="#rust">Environmental hazards</a> complement the player threat — radiation zones in high-loot areas, cold nights requiring warm clothing and fire, animals that attack on encounter, and roaming scientists that guard specific locations add environmental challenge alongside the player-versus-player competition.

<a href="#rust">Persistent server worlds</a> maintain the accumulated state of every player's construction and accumulated materials between sessions — a base built over several play sessions exists when you return, and the server's history of conflicts and alliances accumulates over the server's wipe cycle. <a href="#rust">Regular Facepunch development updates</a> on a monthly cycle add content, balance changes, new craftable items, and new game systems — a game that has evolved substantially since its 2018 full release and continues actively developing.</p>

## 🤔 FAQ

<details>
<summary>Is Rust available on Mac?</summary>

Yes. Rust is available for Mac through Steam and runs natively on supported Mac hardware without emulation.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is Rust a multiplayer-only game?</summary>

Rust is primarily a multiplayer game with persistent server worlds. Solo play is possible but the game is designed around player interaction and conflict.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is a server wipe in Rust?</summary>

A server wipe resets the entire game world — all player bases, items, and accumulated resources are deleted and everyone starts fresh simultaneously. Official servers typically wipe monthly.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How difficult is Rust for new players?</summary>

Rust has a steep learning curve. New players starting on official servers face experienced players with established bases and weapons. Community servers with PvE options or lower population offer more accessible starting experiences.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I play Rust solo?</summary>

Yes, though solo play on large official servers is extremely challenging. Solo-only and duo-only community servers create environments where solo play is more viable.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is raiding in Rust?</summary>

Raiding is when one player or group attacks and destroys another player's base to access and steal the stored resources inside. It is a core and controversial element of Rust gameplay.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Rust have PvE servers without player combat?</summary>

Yes. Community PvE servers disable player-versus-player combat while maintaining the survival, building, and crafting gameplay against environmental threats.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How often does Rust receive updates?</summary>

Facepunch Studios releases Rust updates on a monthly cycle, adding new content, balance changes, and game systems.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are the Mac system requirements for Rust?</summary>

Check the Steam store page for current Rust Mac system requirements, as these update with game development.

</details>
